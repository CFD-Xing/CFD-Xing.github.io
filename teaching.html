Teaching

Vector Calculus and Fluid Mechanics (AER210)

Teaching Assistant
University of Toronto, Toronto, Canada
Sep 2017 – Dec 2018

Job description:

  • Taught lab of microfluidic (2018) and flow visualization (2017) to a total of about 100 undergraduate students, helped students during the session, and marked lab reports

Flow visualization's lab description:

The objective of the laboratory is to observe and describe different flow phenomena, from simple steady flows to more complex unsteady flows, associated with recirculating water in a channel using different model of inserts. The behavior of the flows are observed by introducing air and foam bubbles or by using Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV). The incompressible continuity and the Bernoulli equations are used for the theoretical analysis of the experimental observations. The total duration of the laboratory is three hours. Students must complete and submit a lab report at the end of the session.

Microfluidic's lab description:

The objective of the laboratory is to observe and describe different flow phenomena in various micro channels by analysing "beads". The incompressible continuity equation is used for the theoretical analysis of the experimental observations. The total duration of the laboratory is three hours. Students must complete a 7-8 pages lab report one week after the sessions.

Fluid Dynamics (MEC2200)

Teaching Assistant
Polytechnique Montréal, Montréal, Canada
Sep 2015 – Apr 2016

Job description:

  • Taught fluid dynamic tutorial to a class of 60 undergraduate students, answered students’ questions, and prepared written solutions

Course description:

The following materials are covered: Potential flow, stream functions, Reynolds transport theorem, conservation of mass, momentum, and energy, pipe flow, turbulence, laminar and turbulent boundary layer, dimensional analysis, compressible flows (isentropic flow, shock waves, etc.). Textbook: Fluid Mechanics by Frank M. White .

Course description:
