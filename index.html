Curriculum Vitae

I am currently a PhD candidate at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Prof. C.P.T. Groth. I have a strong interest in CFD and numerical multi-physics modelling. My current research is on the numerical modelling of soot formation in laminar and turbulent diffusion flames. My work notably consisted of the implementation of various soot models in an in-house parallel multi-block finite-volume CFD framework. Additionally, I have also expanded the original laminar code to include turbulence modelling using RANS-based two-equation models for both explicit time-stepping and implicit time-stepping with NKS/GMRES, as well as implemented several tabulation techniques for combustion modelling. Moreover, I have practical experiences with multiple programming languages such as C++, Fortran 90, Python, and MATLAB. Additionally, I am also acquainted with version control software (Git), distributed parallel programming (MPI), and familiar with the development of large-scale CFD codes on Linux operating system. Finally, being involved in a large-scale CFD development, I have experience with supervising junior graduate students and the soot modelling effort involved an important industrial partner (PW&C) and my previous exchange in France also gave me experience regarding collaborating with external researchers.
