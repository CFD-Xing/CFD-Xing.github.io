Research

Improved Soot Modelling for Laminar and Turbulent Flames

Graduate Research Assistant
University of Toronto, Toronto
Sep 2016 – present
  • Developed and implemented quadrature-based moment closure approaches in an in-house 2D parallel multi-block finite-volume framework to improve soot particle modelling predictions in laminar and turbulent non-premixed flames
  • Implemented sectional methods for soot modelling to assess the accuracy of quadrature-based methods of moments in space-homogeneous (0D) problems
  • Implemented Reynolds/Favre-Averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS/FANS) equations in an in-house 2D parallel multi-block finite-volume framework for both explicit and implicit (NKS/GMRES) time-marching approaches
  • Conducted extensive verification and validation of the implementation of several versions (1998, 2006, and SST) of the k-omega turbulence model for a wide range of cases including cases from NASA’s Turbulence Modeling Resource (zero-pressure gradient flat plate and planar coflowing jet), non-reacting bluff body, and non-reacting jet
  • Implemented several chemistry tabulation techniques (FPI, SLFM, and FPV) in an in-house 2D parallel multi-block finite-volume framework for the modelling of turbulence non-premixed flames and performed extensive numerical simulations of a piloted and a lifted turbulent jet flames
  • Improved and expanded chemistry table generation to include non-adiabatic effects and soot chemistry as well as improving the automation of chemistry table generation and user experience
  • Performed a large number of numerical simulations of several laminar and turbulent flames as well as of non-reacting flows using an in-house 2D parallel multi-block finite-volume CFD framework on HPC clusters
  • Assisted and helped several master students in their research activities in matter related to turbulence modelling, chemistry tabulation, soot modelling, and the lab’s in-house CFD code
  • Identified problems related to CFD code development and numerical modelling and suggested solutions to several master students of the research group
  • Programmed with C++, MATLAB, Python, and Fortran; experience with distributed parallel programming (MPI) and version control (Git)

Non-Equilibrium Plasma Modelling

Graduate Research Assistant
Polytechnique Montréal, Montréal
May 2014 – Aug 2016
  • Performed non-equilibrium atmospheric pressure plasma modelling (electrohydrodynamic) for dielectric barrier discharge (DBD) plasma actuators applied to aerodynamic flow control
  • Implemented three species (ions, electrons, and neutrals) and four species (positive ions, negative ions, electrons, and neutrals) drift-diffusion plasma models based on the work of Bœuf et al. 2005 and Bœuf et al. 2008
  • Solved the drift-diffusion equation with the Scharfetter–Gummel exponential scheme and the Poisson equation with Gauss-Seidel and SOR methods
  • Produced aerodynamic flow simulations on a 2D airfoil with plasma actuation using the commercial software Fluent
  • Programmed with MATLAB and Microsoft Visual C++
